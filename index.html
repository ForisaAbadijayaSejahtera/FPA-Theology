FPA - Ecclesiastical Theological Formation Degree System

Theological Formation Degree System for Church Ministry and Leadership Development

A structured ecclesiastical education system designed for equipping Christian leaders, pastors, and ministers for ministry service.

Explore Our System

About System

We operate an ecclesiastical theological formation degree system focused on ministry preparation, leadership development, and biblical training.

This system uses structured academic-style levels within a ministry-based education framework, designed to equip Christian leaders with the knowledge, skills, and spiritual formation necessary for effective church ministry.

Our approach combines rigorous theological study with practical ministry application, ensuring that graduates are thoroughly prepared for the demands of pastoral leadership and ecclesiastical service.

Degree Structure

Three levels of theological formation for comprehensive ministry preparation

Level 1

Bachelor of Theology

(Formation Degree)

Foundation in biblical studies, discipleship, and ministry basics. Designed for those beginning their journey in theological education and ministry preparation.

Level 2

Master of Theology

(Advanced Formation Degree)

Advanced theology, pastoral leadership, and ministry development. For those seeking deeper theological understanding and enhanced leadership capabilities.

Level 3

Doctor of Theology

(Senior Formation Degree)

Advanced ecclesiastical leadership, teaching, and ministry oversight. The highest level of formation for senior church leaders and theological educators.

Important Notice

This is a non-state ecclesiastical education system. It is designed exclusively for:

  • Church ministry formation
  • Christian leadership development
  • Ecclesiastical service training

It is not a civil or government-accredited academic degree system.

Partnership

Where applicable, academic validation or ministry collaboration may be conducted with partner institutions within the ecclesiastical education network. We believe in the strength of collaboration and shared mission in advancing theological education for the church.

Ordination Pathway

Called to Serve

Graduates of the formation system may be considered for ordination into pastoral ministry based on ecclesiastical assessment and leadership affirmation. This pathway ensures that those who serve in church leadership have received proper theological formation and have been recognized by the ecclesiastical community.
